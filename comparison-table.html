Comparison table

Jul-Aug 2020

The cost of insurance and its coverage is the first thing the agent should agree with the client. There can be more than five offers and client only needs to choose one. To make it convenient to compare insurances, we made a page with a table, which contains all the necessary information.

  • – Information architecture
  • – UI/UX Design
  • – Prototyping

Problem

The agents fill out the documents and receive offers on the price of insurance inside the product. Then they need to present it to an insured. To do it they have to manually create a comparison table in Excel. This is extra work that the product can do.

Research

First, we needed to found out what data the insured needs to make a decision. Our Sales team helped us with taking more details from agents. We understood that except for the actual cost and document of the policy, agents needed to describe details such as limits, deductibles, and coverages.

The next step was to compare a dataset for different companies in one insurance line. The data between the two offers can be very different due to the companies settings. We decided that we need a flexible table that would easily adapt to the data.

Solution

We have a special portal for insureds where all of the quotes and policies are storage. We could built a table into the portal so insured will have all the data in one place. It perfectly fits our goals.

For the table we defined a data hierarchy. The most important and unique data is a carrier, document, and cost of the policy. It will be placed on the very top.

The details of the policy are posted below the main info. There can be more than 5 offers and to make it easier to compare the data while horizontal scrolling, the sections’ headers were fixed on the left side. The header with the main info also will be fixed on the top while scrolling.

Outcomes

This page was an important step in the growth of the Insured portal which is one of the main parts of business focus. We tried to create value for the insured by providing useful tool. According to the qualitative data, we see that agents and insureds are interested in this table. But also we knew that there are some problems with inviting insureds to the Insured portal. So for the next iteration, we decided to rethink sharing process↗.
